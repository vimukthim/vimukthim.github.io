Lead Time Reduction
Triumph

Procurement · Customer Connect Project
🎯 Ultimate Goal of the Project

This project was initiated to address a critical strategic risk — extended lead times that were limiting growth, reducing competitiveness, and putting a key customer relationship at risk. At the time, total lead time stood at 175 days, well above market expectations, with a defined gap of 50 days to remain competitive.

I led a structured, data-driven transformation initiative focused on reducing lead time across material ordering and production stages. Through root cause analysis using Lean methodologies, we identified systemic constraints including lack of greige fabric availability, absence of forward booking visibility, supplier limitations, and process inefficiencies within buy planning and BOM finalization.

To address these, we introduced forward looking demand planning mechanisms, aligned supplier and customer accountability on greige commitments, and streamlined internal processes by reducing dependency bottlenecks in SAP BOM readiness. Supplier engagement and negotiation were critical components, particularly in optimizing dyeing and finishing timelines and improving responsiveness.

The initiative delivered a 33% reduction in lead time, unlocking the ability to accept short lead-time orders worth over $150K and securing approximately $5M in ongoing business by meeting customer expectations. Beyond financial gains, the project strengthened customer trust, enhanced supply chain agility, and positioned the organisation as a competitive partner capable of delivering speed-to-market.

This work reflects my capability to lead cross functional transformation, align stakeholders across the value chain, and implement sustainable process improvements that directly impact revenue growth and customer retention.
